How to Participate

  • Create an account on the Eval.ai platform.
  • Download the challenge data for the different challenge tracks indicated in each track.
  • Run your model to obtain predictions for each task included in the challenge track.
  • Upload your results on the challenge page for the validation and/or test phases. Note that only public submissions for the test phase are considered for the final competition.
  • Include an email address with your submission in the test phase.
  • To be eligible for the awards, you must submit a 2-page report in the test phase to ey242@berkeley.edu, describing the model you used to obtain the predictions.
  • All competitors must follow the system and question prompts outlined in the trials.
  • All data provided through the registration process must be complete and correct.

Key Dates

The validation phase for all the challenge tracks begins in June 2025. The test phase begins at 12:00am GMT on 10 July 2025 and ends at 11:59pm GMT on 10 October 2025 (Contest Period).

Determining the Winners

Up to 4 winners will be selected by the KiVA organizers, based on the results obtained by the competitors.

Prizes

  • Up to 4 prizes—each valued at up to $1,000—will be awarded.
  • The prizes will be awarded at the Third Perception Test Challenge workshop organised as a satellite event to the ICCV conference in Hawaii in October 2025.
  • Winners will present their methods in the workshop, in person or online if they cannot join in person.
  • Prizes cannot be awarded to competitors who are:
    • Residents of, or ordinarily resident in, a US embargoed country; or
    • Immediate colleagues and family members of the KiVA organizers.
  • Prizes will be awarded to winners by the Google–Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research Commons Grant.
  • All decisions by the KiVA organizers regarding Prize winners are final.
